How to Prepare for Your Corporate Headshot Session: A Complete Guide

A professional headshot is more than just a photo—it's an investment in your personal brand and career. Whether you're updating your LinkedIn profile, company website, or marketing materials, proper preparation ensures you get the most value from your session.

Wardrobe Selection

Your choice of clothing can significantly impact the success of your headshot. Here are key considerations:

  • Stick to solid colors that complement your skin tone
  • Choose classic, timeless pieces over trendy styles
  • Ensure clothes are well-pressed and fit properly
  • Bring multiple options to your session
  • Avoid busy patterns, loud prints, or distracting logos

Grooming and Preparation

The week before your session:

  • Schedule any haircuts 5-7 days before the shoot
  • Maintain consistent hair color and style
  • Get adequate sleep the night before
  • Stay hydrated and maintain your skincare routine
  • Consider professional makeup application if desired

Posing Tips for Natural Results

During your session, remember these key points:

  • Practice your smile in the mirror beforehand
  • Maintain good posture without appearing stiff
  • Angle your body slightly rather than facing the camera directly
  • Keep your chin slightly forward to define your jawline
  • Take deep breaths to stay relaxed

Technical Considerations

Understanding these aspects helps achieve optimal results:

  • Arrive 10-15 minutes early to settle in
  • Communicate any specific requirements or preferences
  • Discuss image usage and formatting needs
  • Review samples to understand style preferences
  • Ask questions about the retouching process

After the Session

Post-session steps for optimal results:

  • Review your images carefully during selection
  • Consider how each image aligns with your brand
  • Provide specific feedback for retouching
  • Plan for regular updates every 2-3 years
  • Keep both digital and printed copies

Pro Tip

Schedule your session during your peak energy hours. Most people look their best in the morning or early afternoon when they're fresh and alert. Avoid scheduling right after lunch or at the end of a long workday.
